# Chapter 34: 'Comfort Zones'

34 “Comfort Zones” IF AVOIDANCE CAN’T DRIVE YOU COMPLETELY OUT OF THE PROFESsion, it will grudgingly settle for its second favorite outcome: imprisoning you in the dungeon of your so-called comfort zone. Your comfort zone is that dead, gray area in between total failure and signal achievement, as you originally defined it. It is the point at which the pain of remaining at your current level of earnings (which is very real) and the pain of further prospecting (which is objectively imaginary, but no less real to you) arrive at some dark, unconscious equilibrium. You don’t move back, necessarily, but you can’t move forward. You can’t quite afford the home of your dreams; you can’t quite take the family on the real expeditions you’d hoped for (e.g. Kenya and Tanzania, rather than Florida’s drive-through Lion Country Safari); you can’t quite get your super-bright daughter through college without student loans (or into her first-choice school at all, because it wouldn’t come up with enough financial aid); you can’t quite subscribe to the top-tier contribution level of your church’s capital fund. But you look like you’re doing OK. In other words, your pain may be searing, but it isn’t externally visible. Is that any comfort?
