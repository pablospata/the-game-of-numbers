# Chapter 88: Introductions, Not Referrals

TO THOSE OF YOU RE-STARTING YOUR BUSINESS, I HAVE ONE LAST task. (Newbies, put this essential idea in your pocket for another day). Beginning immediately, start prospecting your own book. Call every client you have. Make an appointment to meet with them and zero-base their whole financial plan. Start it all over again, right from the fact-finding stage, as if to introduce The New You—that re-energized, re-invigorated professional who’s now on the case. When you’re done, ask not for a referral; ask for an introduction. Referrals don’t work. Introductions do. Is your client a doctor? Ask him to whom he refers his cardiology cases. Ask him to whom he refers his oncology cases. Tell him in as much detail as you can who you’re looking for; don’t leave it to his imagination. Then ask if he’ll call that person and tell her you’ll be calling. (Even if he demurs, you’ve got a good lead, and he’ll almost certainly let you drop his name.) You deserve introductions. It’s part of your clients’ job description. If you hadn’t made that clear to them before, now’s the time. But that’s not even the point. The point is: introductions, not referrals.
