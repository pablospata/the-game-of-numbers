# Chapter 45: A Valediction from Aristotle

Now, all that remains to be done is habituating you to the work of prospecting, and building up your capacity to prospect, slowly but irreversibly over time. Oddly—once belief and behavior are in place—that turns out to be the easy part.

45 A Valediction from Aristotle PROSPECTING TAKES COURAGE. ALWAYS HAS, ALWAYS WILL, NO matter how habituated to it you become, and no matter how you grow even to enjoy it. I leave you with Aristotle’s essential observation about courage: “Courage is the first of human qualities, because it is the quality that guarantees all the others.” Alexander the Great, perhaps the most courageous warrior of his era, loved Aristotle, who was his teacher. Here, perhaps, you begin to understand why.
