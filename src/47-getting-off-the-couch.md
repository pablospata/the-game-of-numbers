# Chapter 47: Getting Off the Couch

PROSPECTING IS LIKE DISTANCE RUNNING. Almost anyone in reasonably good health, at almost any age, can train to run a marathon. Team in Training, the wildly successful charity sports training program of the Leukemia & Lymphoma Society, has ordinary people running marathons in five months from a standing start, though theirs is a very rigorous training regimen. For purposes of this analogy, this book is being read by two kinds of people. One is those just starting out in the business, who have never prospected/run distances before. The other is those who once used to run distances—perhaps even prospecting marathons—but then stopped, and haven’t run in quite some time. (Permit a slight but important digression, here. Those of you seeking to re-start your prospecting effort after a long layoff may perhaps be inclined to beat yourself up over the fact that you once knew how to prospect—and indeed prospected successfully, up to a point—then stopped, and can’t imagine how to get going again. Like all self-recrimination, this is gratuitously dumb and self-defeating. It’s the shark, whispering that you’ve forgotten how to swim. Nobody, once he knows, ever forgets how to swim. As always, the shark is lying.)
