# Chapter 14: Toward a First Draft

WE BRING THE FIRST SECTION OF THIS BOOK TO A CLOSE WITH A draft belief system. It is admittedly mine, and you have my permission and even encouragement immediately to begin shaping it to yourself: my armor will almost certainly not fit you exactly (nor yours, when it is finished, me). But if you find it insufficiently detailed, stop dead in your tracks. Because you may already have begun to miss the point, and you mustn’t try to read on. Of course it’s not very detailed. It is, instead, deliberately essential. It frames up a newly knighted advisor’s approach to the overwhelming portion of all financial planning, insurance and investments that’s just common sense. It is not interested in the refinements precisely because they are refinements. You will—because there is probably no way I can stop you—rush ahead to the refinements soon enough. And find them ultimately to be some combination of unknowable, immaterial and irrelevant. Then, like Napoleon turning back from the gates of Moscow, you will have to stagger through the bitter snows toward home, there to rediscover the fundamental truth that—as the immortal Leonardo assured us—“Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.”
