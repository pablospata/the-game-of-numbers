# Chapter 52: It's Not the Level; It's the Trajectory

51 Raising the Baseline UPON COMPLETING TWO WEEKS AT THE SAME LEVEL OF EFFORT AT the same time every training day—allowing nothing to interfere— you may have found your baseline level of running/prospecting activity. (Then again you may not, but you’ve made an excellent start.) As we just saw, it may take you a couple or more attempts to establish your baseline. I say again: so be it. There’s no rush. And above all, as you’re searching for your baseline, there must be no self-recrimination. I know I said this barely a moment ago, but I can’t overstress it. You simply cannot swim away from the shark if one of your arms is engaged in the act of beating yourself up. But once you’ve established a performance level you can sustain for two weeks, the next step is to raise, very slightly, the level of effort. For purposes of this scientific experiment, let’s make the increment 10%. So if you were jogging three tenths of a mile at four and a half miles per hour, you’ll try a third of a mile for two weeks. If you were calling the phone numbers of 20 plumbing and heating contractors, you’ll dial 22. If you were knocking on 10 doors, you’ll knock on 11. And you’ll see if you can sustain that level every training/prospecting day for two weeks. And so on.
